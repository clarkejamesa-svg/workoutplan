Training Tips

For explosive training at 40

Quality over quantity

Explosive reps require full recovery between sets. Don't rush rest periods — power output per rep matters more than volume.

Sleep is the main recovery tool

Aim for 7-9 hours on training nights. At 40, adaptation happens during sleep more than any other time.

Protein intake

Target 1.6-2.0g of protein per kg of bodyweight daily. Muscles need the raw material to rebuild after explosive work.

Listen to your joints

Muscle soreness is normal. Joint discomfort means rest. Tendons take longer to adapt than muscles at any age.

Progress by quality, not just load

Every 2-3 weeks aim to increase box height or jump distance rather than adding more reps.

Neuromuscular adaptation is fast

You may feel stronger within 2-3 weeks even before visible muscle change — that's the nervous system learning to fire harder.

Hockey day rule

Never do legs the day before hockey. Your training is always scheduled to protect your on-ice performance.
